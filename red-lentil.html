Turkish Red Lentil Soup

Description:

Turkish Red Lentil Soup (Mercimek Çorbası) is a wholesome and comforting dish, perfect for any season. Made with red lentils, vegetables, and fragrant spices like cumin and paprika, this hearty soup is rich in flavor and easy to prepare. It’s a staple in Turkish cuisine, often served with a squeeze of lemon and a sprinkle of fresh parsley. Whether you're looking for a quick meal or a nutritious dish to warm you up, this soup is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and grated or finely chopped
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and chopped (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried mint (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth (or water)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional, for serving)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil or butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until soft and translucent. Add the grated carrot and potato (if using), and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add the Tomato Paste and Spices: Stir in the tomato paste, cumin, paprika, and dried mint (if using). Cook for 1-2 minutes to allow the spices to release their flavors.
  3. Cook the Lentils: Add the rinsed red lentils to the pot, followed by the vegetable or chicken broth (or water). Stir everything together and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the lentils are fully cooked and soft.
  4. Blend the Soup (Optional): Once the lentils are tender, you can either leave the soup chunky or use an immersion blender to puree the soup for a smooth texture. You can also transfer the soup to a blender in batches if you don’t have an immersion blender.
  5. Adjust the Consistency: If the soup is too thick, add a little more broth or water to reach your desired consistency.
  6. Season: Taste the soup and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness.
  7. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls, garnish with fresh parsley (if desired), and serve with a wedge of lemon on the side for extra flavor.
